# Design Direction

Wish I Knew should feel like a playful Australian parenting adventure map with warm Saturday-morning-coffee energy.

## Reference Mood

The current creative direction blends:

- cosy indie-game charm
- soft pixel-inspired detail
- modern app polish
- Australian coastal and suburban warmth
- collectible card delight
- calm parenting reassurance

Internal shorthand: **NSW parenting adventure plus clean neo-bank confidence**.

Do not copy protected art direction, game visuals, logos, layouts or brand systems. Dave the Diver, Up Bank, Pokemon and Bluey are mood references only, not visual sources to imitate.

## Brand Vibe

- Playful: adventurous like NSW families discovering what comes next.
- Warm: fun and curious without being childish.
- Reassuring: emotionally intelligent and calm.
- Smart: helpful information delivered simply.

## Visual Principles

- Cards should feel collectible and useful.
- The world should feel Australian to locals without becoming tourism cliche.
- The UI should be colourful without becoming noisy.
- Parents should understand the next useful thing in under 10 seconds.
- Safety and admin content should still feel trustworthy.
- Artwork should use warm cream, ocean teal, coral, sun yellow, gum green and jacaranda accents.

## Source Of Truth

The detailed image rules, palette, prompt templates and review checklist live in `docs/card-image-guidelines.md`.

## Current Asset Approach

The app uses original SVG illustrations under `public/card-images/placeholders/`.

These are intentionally simple MVP assets. They should later be replaced or expanded through the approved card image workflow, but they satisfy the product rule that published cards are visual.
