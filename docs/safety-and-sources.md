# Safety And Sources

Wish I Knew must be trustworthy and calm.

It must not diagnose, replace professional advice, imply every child develops to an exact schedule, or tell parents to ignore a GP, midwife, child and family health nurse, lactation consultant, dietitian or emergency service.

## Sensitive Content

Cards are sensitive if they involve:

- medical topics
- feeding
- allergy
- immunisation
- safe sleep
- safety
- government payments or subsidy
- legal/admin obligations

Sensitive published cards require:

- source URL or source note
- last reviewed date
- review due date

## Preferred Australian Sources

- health.gov.au
- Services Australia
- Pregnancy Birth and Baby
- Raising Children Network
- state and territory health departments
- ASCIA
- Red Nose
- Australian Breastfeeding Association

Use cautious wording: "Around this stage", "many parents", "ask your GP or child and family health nurse", and "check official guidance".
